STEPS

Scalable Training Estimation and Planning System for FETP India

Why STEPS?

STEPS is a decision aid designed to help National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) and partners explore scalable training plans that strengthen and expand the Field Epidemiology Training Programme (FETP) in India. STEPS combines a preference model, indicative costing and a simple epidemiological module to provide transparent scenario comparisons.

Preference model

Supports scenario design by predicting endorsement and perceived programme value.

Costing transparency

Estimates direct and economic costs, with optional opportunity costs, to support cost-benefit assessments.

Epidemiological module

Provides indicative benefits from improved outbreak response capacity, linked to graduates produced over the planning horizon.

Policy-ready exports

Save scenarios, compare side by side and export Excel and PDF summaries for meetings and reports.

Note: Preferences and perceived programme value estimates (derived from a preference study) come from a discrete choice experiment. Perceived programme value is an implicit rupee valuation derived from stated preferences in the discrete choice experiment. It is not an actual payment requirement.

Policy and training framework

STEPS represents the main policy levers and delivery features that can be adjusted to scale up FETP in India. Use this tab to remind users of the attribute definitions used in the preference study and the operational levers represented in the configuration panel.

Programme tier

  • Intermediate – shorter training, focused on core outbreak response.
  • Advanced – longer training, higher skills and leadership focus.

Career incentives

  • Limited – current incentives.
  • Enhanced – clearer pathways to promotion and recognition.

Mentorship intensity

  • Low – lighter mentor engagement.
  • Medium – standard mentor engagement.
  • High – intensive mentor engagement.

Delivery mode

  • In-person – higher contact delivery.
  • Blended – mix of in-person and online delivery.
  • Online – fully remote delivery.

Response time

  • Standard – current mobilisation timeline.
  • Rapid – improved readiness and deployment.

Cost

  • Cost is expressed as rupees per trainee per month.
  • Use settings to adjust costs, discount rates and other assumptions.

Perceived programme value and economic outcomes

Perceived programme value per trainee per month -

The implicit valuation per trainee per month derived from stated preferences. Higher values mean higher perceived value.

Total perceived programme value per cohort -

Aggregated implicit valuation for one cohort (not a budget or payment requirement).

Perceived programme value is an implicit valuation derived from stated preferences in the discrete choice experiment. It is not an actual payment requirement and should be interpreted as a support and value signal from the preference study.

Training delivery cost per cohort (excl. mentor) -

Direct delivery cost for one cohort (before mentor support and opportunity cost).

Mentor support cost per cohort -

Explicit mentor support component for one cohort.

Direct cost per cohort (incl. mentor) -

Direct financial cost for one cohort (excluding opportunity cost).

Total economic cost per cohort -

Direct cost plus opportunity cost where applicable. This is the main cost figure used in benefit cost ratios.

Net benefit per cohort (epidemiological) -

This shows whether the health benefits from better outbreak response are greater than the costs for one cohort of trainees.

Benefit cost ratio (per cohort) -

A comparison of outbreak benefits to costs for one cohort. Values above 1 mean benefits outweigh costs.

Annual outbreak responses that could be improved through the trained network.

Total outbreak response benefit (discounted, INR) -

Indicative discounted value of outbreak response improvements over the full planning horizon.

Briefing prompts for Copilot and ChatGPT

Use this tab to generate a policy-ready prompt that you can paste into Copilot or ChatGPT. The prompt automatically includes the current configuration, saved scenarios (top three) and key assumptions.

Click one of the buttons to refresh and copy the prompt, then paste it into Copilot or ChatGPT in a separate tab. The generated response can be used directly in meeting notes, a 2 page policy brief or slide-ready summary.
